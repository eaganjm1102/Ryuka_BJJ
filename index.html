Our History

Ryuka Jiu Jitsu was born from the rich tradition of Hawaii Judo Academy (HJA). While our Jiu Jitsu competitors have long represented HJA with honor and excellence, we recognized the need to showcase our dedicated Brazilian Jiu Jitsu program.

Our Brazilian Jiu Jitsu Lineage

Our Brazilian Jiu Jitsu lineage traces back through generations of legendary masters, each contributing to the evolution and refinement of the gentle art. This direct lineage ensures authentic technique and traditional values.

Helio Gracie
Co-Founder of Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (1913-2009)
Helio Gracie co-founded Brazilian Jiu Jitsu alongside his brother Carlos. Known for adapting traditional Japanese jujitsu techniques to favor leverage and technique over strength, Helio revolutionized martial arts. He competed in legendary matches and established the foundation of modern BJJ, emphasizing that a smaller, weaker person could defend against larger opponents through proper technique.
Rickson Gracie
Son of Helio Gracie, Undefeated Champion
Rickson Gracie is widely considered one of the greatest Brazilian Jiu Jitsu practitioners of all time. With an alleged undefeated record of over 400 fights, Rickson dominated both BJJ and mixed martial arts competitions. Known for his impeccable technique, breathing methods, and mental approach to combat, he elevated the art to new levels and trained champions worldwide.
Fabio Santos
Black Belt under Rickson Gracie
Fabio Santos is a renowned Brazilian Jiu Jitsu black belt under Rickson Gracie, known for his technical precision and teaching ability. Having trained directly under the Gracie family, Fabio has dedicated his life to preserving and passing on authentic Brazilian Jiu Jitsu techniques. He has produced numerous champions and continues to influence the art through his teaching methodology.
Mike Fowler
Black Belt under Fabio Santos
Mike Fowler is a respected Brazilian Jiu Jitsu black belt who trained under Fabio Santos, carrying forward the traditional Gracie lineage. Known for his dedication to technique and his ability to develop students at all levels, Mike has been instrumental in spreading authentic BJJ throughout his teaching career. His approach emphasizes the fundamental principles that make Brazilian Jiu Jitsu so effective.
Chris Nitta
Head Instructor, Ryuka Jiu Jitsu
Sensei Chris Nitta is the head instructor at Ryuka Jiu Jitsu and continues this prestigious lineage in Hawaii. Training under Mike Fowler, Chris brings authentic Gracie Jiu Jitsu to Hawaii Judo Academy. His teaching combines traditional techniques with modern applications, ensuring students receive the highest quality instruction rooted in proven methodology. Chris is dedicated to developing both the technical and character aspects of his students.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need experience to start?

Not at all! Our fundamentals classes are designed for complete beginners. We'll teach you everything you need to know from day one.

What should I wear to my first class?

Comfortable athletic clothes (t-shirt and shorts/leggings). We'll provide a gi for your first class if you decide to continue training.

Is Brazilian Jiu Jitsu safe?

Yes! BJJ is one of the safest martial arts when practiced properly. Our instructors emphasize safety and controlled training at all times.

How often should I train?

We recommend 2-3 times per week for optimal progress, but you can start with once a week and increase as you build conditioning and technique.
